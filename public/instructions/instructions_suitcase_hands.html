Instructions

Human-object contact points are the usual places on an object where a human touches an object when they interacts with it in a particular action mode. Different action modes can potentially mean different contact points for the same object. In this task we ask you to highlight contact points on an object for a particular action. Below are example correct clicks for hand contact points for a human carrying a suitcase. Note that your goal is to click on typical hand contact points (i.e., suitcase hand-holders) even if they are occluded by a human hand, or a person is touching a suitcase at a different region than the usual contact-points, or if there is no person at all.

Click on hand-contact points of the suitcase in each image



If contact points are occluded (e.g., covered up by hand) still click on the most likely location in the image.



If the person is not interacting with a suitcase, still click on typical contact points if a person were to interact with a suitcase (i.e., suitcase handles in the example above)


Click on typical contact points even if there is no contact between the two.


If the contact points are unclear, out of frame, or you cannot select them for any other reason, please check the Cannot Complete box. Please only use this option as a final resort. Erroneously clicking this box means you will NOT get paid. Very few images, if any, should have this box clicked on.
